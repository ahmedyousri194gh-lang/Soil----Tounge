Problem

Many farmers face challenges because they do not know the soil conditions well. Without accurate information about the soil, it is difficult to decide which crop to plant or how to take care of it. This problem can lead to low crop production, poor plant health, and sometimes complete crop failure. Soil has different properties such as pH, moisture, salinity, and temperature, all of which are very important for plant growth. If these factors are not suitable, plants cannot absorb nutrients properly, grow slowly, or may even die. For example, planting a crop in soil with the wrong pH can prevent the plant from taking essential minerals. Similarly, unsuitable temperature or too little water can harm the plant. Many farmers do not have tools to measure these conditions easily, so they often rely on experience or guesswork. This is why a simple device that measures soil properties and suggests the right crop is very useful.

Causes of poor plant growth:

  1. Wrong pH level for the crop
  2. Planting in soil with unsuitable pH
  3. Temperature not suitable for growth

Solution

The problem of not knowing the soil is very common for farmers. The solution is a simple smart device that can check the soil. This device measures important things like pH, moisture, salt, and temperature. By knowing these values, farmers can choose the right crop for their soil. This stops problems like bad growth or plants dying.

The device shows the results on a small screen. It is easy to read and understand. After measuring, the device can also tell which crop is best for the soil. This saves time, reduces losses, and helps farmers grow more food. Farmers do not need to guess or depend only on experience.

Using this device also helps the environment and farming. Planting the right crop for the soil uses water and fertilizer better. This protects the soil and makes farming safe and healthy.

Material

  • Arduino board
  • pH sensor
  • Soil moisture sensor
  • Soil temperature sensor (DS18B20)
  • Salinity (TDS) sensor
  • LCD screen (16x2 with I2C)
  • Breadboard
  • Wooden board for mounting sensors
  • Battery / Power bank
  • Jumper wires

These components are low-cost and widely available. The system is designed to be simple to assemble and maintain — great for both smallholder and experimental use.

Soil Types

Sandy Soil

Drains water very fast, warms quickly, low nutrients, good for plants that need dry soil.

Clay Soil

Holds water for a long time, heavy and dense, rich in nutrients, but drains slowly.

Loamy Soil

Best soil for most crops, balanced mix of sand, silt, and clay, holds water well and drains well.

Silty Soil

Smooth and soft texture, holds water better than sandy soil, more nutrients, can compact easily.

How to Use the Project

1. Place the sensors in the soil carefully.
2. Turn on the device using the battery or power bank.
3. Wait a few seconds for the sensors to read the soil values.
4. Check the LCD screen to see the pH, moisture, temperature, and salinity.
5. Look at the recommended crop that appears on the screen.
6. Use the information to choose the best plant for your soil.

How to improve soil

  • Add lime to reduce acidity.
  • Add sulfur to reduce alkalinity.
  • Add animal manure or compost to increase nutrients.
  • Use wood chips to retain moisture.
  • Improve drainage and loosen the soil to help roots breathe.
